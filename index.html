Esterification

HKDSE Topic 11

Concepts: Synthesis of Esters

Aim & Principle

Esterification is a condensation reaction where an alkanol reacts with an alkanoic acid to form an ester and water. It is a slow, reversible reaction.

\(\text{Alkanoic Acid} + \text{Alkanol} \rightleftharpoons \text{Ester} + \text{Water}\)

Key Conditions:

  • Heating under reflux: Speeds up the reaction without losing volatile organic reactants/products.
  • Concentrated Sulphuric Acid (\(\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4\)): Acts as a catalyst to speed up the reaction, and as a dehydrating agent. By removing water, it shifts the equilibrium position to the right (Le Chatelier's Principle), increasing the yield of the ester.

Naming Esters (Nomenclature)

Esters have a characteristic fruity smell. Their names consist of two parts:

  1. The first part comes from the alkanol (ends in -yl).
  2. The second part comes from the alkanoic acid (ends in -oate).
Alkanoic Acid Alkanol Ester Name Equation
Ethanoic acid Ethanol Ethyl ethanoate \(\text{CH}_3\text{COOH} + \text{C}_2\text{H}_5\text{OH} \rightleftharpoons \text{CH}_3\text{COOC}_2\text{H}_5 + \text{H}_2\text{O}\)
Methanoic acid Propan-1-ol Propyl methanoate \(\text{HCOOH} + \text{C}_3\text{H}_7\text{OH} \rightleftharpoons \text{HCOOC}_3\text{H}_7 + \text{H}_2\text{O}\)

The Reaction Mechanism (Micro-view)

During esterification, a molecule of water is eliminated. Crucially, studies using isotopic labelling (Oxygen-18) prove that:

  • The -OH group is removed from the Alkanoic Acid.
  • The -H atom is removed from the Alkanol.

Process Flowchart: Making an Ester

Step 1: Mixing Reactants

Mix equal volumes of the alkanol (e.g., ethanol) and alkanoic acid (e.g., ethanoic acid) in a pear-shaped flask.

Step 2: Adding Catalyst & Safety Measures

Carefully add a few drops of concentrated sulphuric acid (\(\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4\)). Add a few anti-bumping granules.

Safety / DSE Key Point: Anti-bumping granules ensure smooth boiling and prevent explosive boiling (bumping) by providing a surface for small bubbles to form.

Step 3: Heating under Reflux

Attach a Liebig condenser vertically to the flask. Heat the mixture using an oil bath or heating mantle for about 15-30 minutes.

Condenser Rule: Water must enter from the bottom and exit from the top to ensure the cooling jacket is completely filled with water.
Safety: Do NOT use a naked flame (Bunsen burner) because organics (alcohols, esters) are highly flammable.

Step 4: Separation (Distillation)

Rearrange the apparatus for simple distillation to distill off the crude ester, which has a lower boiling point than the reactants.

Step 5: Purification (Using Separating Funnel)

Wash the distillate with sodium carbonate solution (\(\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3\)) in a separating funnel to remove unreacted acid.

DSE Key Point: Open the stopcock frequently during shaking to release \(\text{CO}_2\) gas pressure generated by the reaction between the acid and carbonate.

Answers & Corrections

Common Misconceptions

Common Error

"In esterification, the water molecule is formed from the -OH of the alcohol and the -H of the carboxylic acid."

Correct Concept

The -OH comes from the alkanoic acid, and the -H comes from the alkanol. This is proven using Oxygen-18 isotopic labeling.

Common Error

"Heating under reflux is used to distill and collect the ester immediately as it forms."

Correct Concept

Reflux prevents the loss of volatile reactants and products by condensing them back into the flask, allowing prolonged heating to reach equilibrium. It does not separate the ester. Distillation is done after reflux.

Common Error

"The product of propanol and ethanoic acid is propyl ethanoate, written as \(\text{C}_3\text{H}_7\text{COOCH}_3\)."

Correct Concept

The structural formula is written with the acid part first. It is propyl ethanoate, but the formula is \(\text{CH}_3\text{COOC}_3\text{H}_7\) or \(\text{CH}_3\text{COOCH}_2\text{CH}_2\text{CH}_3\).

Revision Summary

Core Facts

  • Reaction: Alkanol + Alkanoic acid \(\rightleftharpoons\) Ester + Water
  • Type: Condensation / Reversible
  • Catalyst: Concentrated \(\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4\)
  • Mechanism: Acid loses -OH, Alcohol loses -H.
  • Physical Property: Esters have sweet, fruity smells and are insoluble in water.

Lab Techniques

  • Reflux: Heats mixture without losing volatile substances.
  • Heating: Water bath/heating mantle (flammable organics).
  • Anti-bumping granules: Prevent explosive boiling.
  • Condenser water flow: Bottom IN, Top OUT.
  • Purification: Wash with \(\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3(aq)\) to neutralize excess acid; release \(\text{CO}_2\) gas.
